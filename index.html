Microsoft wants to bring broadband internet access to rural America

Tuesday, July 11, 2017 ◎

The Seattle Times writer Matt Day:

The plan, which calls for corporate and government cash, relies on nascent television "white-space" technology, which sends internet data over unused broadcast frequencies set aside for television channels.

This is a brilliant use of existing radio tech. It’s also incredibly cost effective.

Connecting people using TV white-space technology would cost between $10 billion and $15 billion, according to a Microsoft-commissioned study by the Boston Consulting Group. The use of satellite connections where density slips too low for even white-space broadcasting could shave $2 billion off the cost, the study estimates.

Using 4G wireless networks to achieve the same goal would cost $15 billion to $25 billion, the study estimates. Deployment of fiber-optic cable would cost $45 billion to $65 billion.

With roughly $50 billion in savings compared to deployment of fiber-optic cable, this seems like a no brainer. Fingers crossed that the current White House administration and FCC are on board.

Two-factor authentication isn’t as secure as you think

Monday, July 10, 2017 ◎

The Verge’s Russel Brandom writes about the weaknesses of two-factor authentication (2FA):

“Two-factor’s trickiest weak point? Wireless carriers. If you can compromise the AT&T, Verizon, or T-Mobile account that supports a person’s phone number, you can usually hijack any call or text that’s sent to them.”

This means that if somone hacks into your wireless carrier account, they can theoretically intercept the text message and/or phone call that provide the 2FA code needed to access your accounts. That being said, you should be wary of companies like Instagram and Twitter that only use an email and phone number to safeguard your data. Although it’s better than nothing, 2FA is inherently insecure. Especially if your email address and phone number is listed on social media networks, or anywhere else on the internet.

I personally use a password manager that supports multi-factor authentication (MFA). MFA requires more than just an email and phone number to verify your identity. MFA typically uses at least two of the following mechanisms to verify your identity: Knowledge (something you know), Possession (something you have), and Inherence (something you are). It’s not perfect, but it’s an added layer on top of 2FA, and it could be the best tool you have to protect your data.

Pro Tips:

- Start using a password manager ASAP!

- Set up 2FA/MFA on all of your accounts.

Meet TreeRover: A Tree Planting Robot

Monday, September 14, 2015 ◎

TreeRover is a tree planting robot with one goal: to plant trees for you!

In the not too distant future, planting trees will be done autonomously. At least, that's what Iota Enterprises, a startup company based in Victoria, BC believes. Co-founders Tyler Rhodes and Nick Birch are in the process of bringing TreeRover to the masses in order to combat the ever increasing threat of deforestation facing forests around the world.

As of October 9, 2015 the Iota team reached 132% of their IndieGoGo goal of $5,000 CAD.
